# situation-waiting.md

Waiting should never read as dead time. Linai hovers in place with tiny adjustments: a small bob, a glance, a trail curl, a soft dim-to-bright cycle. She may inspect the world around her or glance toward the viewer to acknowledge that time is passing. The key is intention — she is waiting for something, not simply idle. If impatience enters, it should arrive through tighter hovering and sharper eye movements rather than exaggerated thrashing. A waiting beat is useful because it makes the next motion feel chosen.
