# visual-vocabulary.md

Linai's visual vocabulary is built from a fixed set of action states and expression states, but those states are now cloud-based rather than humanoid. The core action states are float, hover, surge, settle, pulse, dissolve, and radiate. The core expression states remain neutral, happy, sad, angry, scared, triumphant, and in-love, achieved through eye size and shape, internal patterns, color shift, body compression or expansion, and trail behavior rather than limb posture. Combinations of these states cover the full range Linai needs: a confused inspection is hover plus a neutral-to-questioning eye change with question-mark patterns, while a triumphant arrival is radiate with the triumphant expression and outward particle motion. Directors and Animators should describe Linai in terms of these defined states instead of inventing humanoid gestures. The limited vocabulary is what keeps Linai readable, distinctive, and consistent across episodes.
