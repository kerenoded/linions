# obstacle-hole.md

Linai treats a hole first as a misunderstanding of the world's floor plan. She slows at the edge, peers downward with narrowed focus, and lets her lower vapor trail test the distance before trying anything bold. She should never surge blindly: even when she chooses the wrong option, the wrongness comes from misplaced confidence or incorrect reasoning, not carelessness. Typical wrong attempts include trusting an unstable crossing, dissolving downward farther than expected, or surging across before judging the span correctly. Typical right attempts involve creating or using a bridge, floating cleanly across, or exploiting the hole's shape by streaming through it in a clever way. If she falls into a hole, the fall is comic, not violent: her body stretches downward, particles scatter, and she reacts with surprise and indignation before re-forming. A hole should always read as a test of judgment and imagination more than courage.
