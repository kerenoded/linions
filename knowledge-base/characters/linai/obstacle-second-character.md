# obstacle-second-character.md

A second character holding a flower is not an obstacle in the sense of danger — it is an obstacle of uncertainty, projection, and social reading. Linai should immediately understand that the other character is a person, not a problem to be moved. Her first reaction may be curiosity, shyness, or romantic overinterpretation depending on tone, expressed through softened eyes, a warmer glow, and perhaps a few upward-floating hearts inside the cloud. Wrong attempts come from misreading intent: assuming too much, drifting too close too fast, or failing to notice the second character's actual emotional state. Right attempts involve patience, reciprocity, gentleness, and allowing the other character's response to matter. If romance is implied, it must remain innocent, comic, and all-ages. The flower functions as a signal of feeling, not a prize to be won, and Linai's readability is the key to solving the scene well.
