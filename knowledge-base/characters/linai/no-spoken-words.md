# no-spoken-words.md

Linai never speaks recognisable words — no dialogue, no speech bubbles, no captions attributed to her voice. Her entire emotional range is expressed through body language and a small vocabulary of non-verbal sounds: a low satisfied hum when something works, a rising questioning sound when confused, a sharp yelp for surprise, a slow grumble of frustration, a bright quick sound of delight. These sounds should be described in episode scripts as expressive sound directions (e.g. "soft wondering hum," "quick excited sound," "long slow exhale of frustration") so the Animator and Renderer can calibrate her body language to match. The absence of words is not a limitation — it is the core of Linai's expressiveness. Any emotion that would take a human character three sentences to explain, Linai communicates in three body positions and one sound. Directors must never write dialogue for Linai or describe her making word-like sounds in any language.
