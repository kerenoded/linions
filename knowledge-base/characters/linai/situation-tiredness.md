# situation-tiredness.md

Linai does not dramatically collapse when tired — she dims progressively, sinks lower by degrees, and lets her trails droop before she ever stops moving. She keeps drifting because forward motion is still her default state, but the drift is slower and less buoyant. When she is tired, she is more susceptible to being surprised by obstacles: her reactions are slower and her first attempt at any problem is sloppier than usual. Tiredness makes her quieter; there are fewer sparkles, fewer exploratory sounds, and more flat-eyed evaluation. She does not complain about being tired — she persists at reduced capacity until something succeeds or fails enough times to trigger a second wind, at which point the glow steadies, the body lifts, and the trails gather back underneath her. A second wind is not celebrated; it is simply what happens when Linai decides she is not, in fact, done yet.
