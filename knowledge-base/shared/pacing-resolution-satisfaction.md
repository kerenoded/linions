# pacing-resolution-satisfaction.md

The resolution beat after a successful choice should feel earned and satisfying, not rushed. After the winning action executes, there is a beat of outcome animation — the obstacle moves, the crossing happens, the solution clicks into place — followed by the character's reaction and celebration. The resolution clip's total duration is typically four to six seconds: long enough to land but short enough to keep momentum. A failed resolution is equally complete: the failure plays out fully, the character reacts, and the retry path begins — this entire sequence should also be four to six seconds so the viewer does not feel punished with a long failure sequence. After either resolution, the transition to the next approach float should be smooth and almost immediate: the character is already drifting before the scene fully settles. The episode's emotional momentum carries forward from resolution into approach, so if the character just won they float brighter and wider, and if they just failed they float with slightly tighter determination.
