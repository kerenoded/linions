# structure-multi-act-escalation.md

A two- or three-act episode should have a sense of escalating stakes even though no act is presented as dangerous. The first act introduces the episode's tone — a gentle, manageable obstacle that establishes the character's personality and problem-solving style. The second act is more complex: the obstacle is larger, the choices are less obvious, and the fail path is more elaborate. The third act, if present, is the episode's peak: the obstacle feels most improbable, the stakes feel highest within the warm comic register, and the winning resolution is the most satisfying. Between acts the approach floats carry forward the emotional state from the previous resolution: a string of wins makes the character progressively brighter and bolder, while a mix of wins and fails makes determination visible in the eye focus, trail tension, and body shape. The overall episode arc should end on an upward note — the final act's winning path should feel like the reward the whole episode was building toward.
