# tone-all-ages.md

Linions episodes are suitable for viewers of all ages, including very young children. This means: no violence, no threatening characters, no frightening outcomes, no uncomfortable situations that are not immediately resolved. A fail path may include the character falling, getting wet, or sliding back down something — but they are never hurt, never in danger, and never humiliated in a way that feels mean. The comedy is universal: physical comedy, timing comedy, and the comedy of persistence against improbable circumstances translate across age groups and cultures. The visual style — expressive movement, vivid backgrounds, no text except choice labels — makes episodes accessible to viewers who cannot read. Directors should assume the youngest possible viewer when assessing whether any moment in an episode crosses a line: if a five-year-old would find it distressing rather than funny, redesign the moment. The warmth of the tone is non-negotiable — this is the single most important stylistic constraint in the entire knowledge base.
