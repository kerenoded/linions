# obstacles-are-absurd.md

Every obstacle in the Linions world is absurd by definition: it exists in the middle of nowhere, on a line that crosses an otherwise featureless landscape, with no explanation for its presence. This absurdity is never addressed or questioned by the narrative — it is simply accepted. A wall is there. A tree is growing on the line. A bird has decided to sit precisely here. The episode does not explain why; the character does not ask why. The absurdity is the comedy: obstacles are not frightening because their existence is so fundamentally improbable that fear would be the wrong reaction. Directors should lean into the obstacle's inherent inexplicability — the funnier the obstacle's implied origin, the better the episode feels. The second character holding a flower is particularly absurd because their purpose is unclear until it resolves: are they a gift-giver? A fellow traveller? An obstacle? All three are true simultaneously. The obstacle's absurdity does not reduce the main character's seriousness in addressing it — they take every obstacle completely seriously, which is the joke.
