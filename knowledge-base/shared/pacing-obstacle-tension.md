# pacing-obstacle-tension.md

The moment the character encounters an obstacle, the episode's pace shifts from relaxed to tense — not frightening, but focused. Their float slows to a hover, they assess, and the viewer knows something must now be resolved. This tension is maintained through the character's careful deliberation: they do not rush their failed attempts, and each one is given full space to play out before the next begins. The choice moment, when the viewer is asked to decide, represents peak tension: the scene holds and the character's acting tightens around the problem. The choice buttons appearing is a visual and narrative release of that tension; the decision now belongs to the viewer. Tension should never feel oppressive or stressful — it is the pleasant tension of a puzzle being presented, not a danger being survived. The longest this phase should run is approximately twelve seconds of animation before the viewer choice: more than that and the episode starts to feel slow.
