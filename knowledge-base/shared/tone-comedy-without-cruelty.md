# tone-comedy-without-cruelty.md

The comedy in Linions episodes comes from situation, timing, and the character's disproportionate reactions — never from humiliation, misfortune that lasts, or cruelty to any character. A pratfall is funny because of the unexpected completeness of it, not because the character is hurt. An obstacle is funny because of its absurd presence, not because it is punishing. The second character is never mocked and never the butt of the joke — any comedy involving another character comes from misunderstanding or miscommunication, resolved warmly. Objects that fail are simply objects; they have no malice. The world is absurd but not hostile: the wall is in the middle of nowhere because that is simply where walls are in this world, not to torment the character specifically. The viewer who chooses the fail path is not punished for their choice — the fail is as well-crafted and complete as the win, just differently satisfying. The tone should feel like a gentle shrug at the universe's inexplicable arrangements, made by someone who finds the universe fundamentally interesting.
