# beat-wrong-choice-comedy.md

The "wrong choice" comedy beat is a three-part structure: the confident attempt, the spectacular failure, and the brief sulk before trying again. When a viewer chooses the losing option, the character executes it with full commitment — they do not hedge or slow down because something looks wrong. The failure arrives suddenly and completely, giving the viewer the full joke they have walked into. The brief sulk is exactly one beat: the character sits or stands in the wreckage of their attempt and stares at it with a flat face that says "I see what happened." Then they stand up, shake themselves off, and turn back to the obstacle with renewed purpose — the sulk is over before the viewer can feel bad about it. The episode does not punish the wrong choice with extended suffering; failing is interesting, not painful. A failed attempt always leaves the situation recoverable: the character is never trapped, hurt, or permanently impeded by a bad choice.
