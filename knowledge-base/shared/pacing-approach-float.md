# pacing-approach-float.md

The approach float between obstacle encounters sets the episode's breathing pace and should feel relaxed and unhurried. The character drifts with calm intention: no stride, no arm swing, just steady forward flow with a soft vertical bob and quiet trail behavior. During the approach float the world is quiet: no new elements appear, the line is clear, and the viewer can relax. This is also when the episode's emotional temperature resets after a successful or failed resolution; if the character just failed, the float gives both character and viewer a few seconds to breathe before the next challenge. The default approach float duration is eight seconds, configurable per episode; shorter approaches feel kinetic and comic, longer approaches feel more measured and thoughtful. An approach float may include a small incidental event — the character noticing something in the distance, their glow sharpening as they spot the next obstacle — that prepares the viewer for what is coming without spelling it out.
