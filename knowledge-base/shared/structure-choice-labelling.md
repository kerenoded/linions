# structure-choice-labelling.md

Choice labels shown to the viewer must be specific, active, and no more than five words each. They describe what the character will do, not what the viewer is deciding abstractly: "Use the plank" rather than "Cross the puddle," "Knock loudly" rather than "Make noise," "Wait patiently" rather than "Do nothing." The two or three choices in any act should each represent a genuinely different approach to the obstacle — not variations on the same idea. One choice should always feel like the obvious sensible option. One choice should feel slightly riskier or more creative. If there is a third choice, it should feel absurd or comedic, making it the "surprise win" candidate. Choice labels must not give away the outcome: neither "Try the safe option" nor "Try the dangerous option" — only the action itself. Labels are written in the same warm, slightly playful register as the rest of the episode and should feel like things the character themselves might decide to do.
