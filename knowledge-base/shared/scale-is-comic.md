# scale-is-comic.md

Scale in the Linions world is relative and deliberately inconsistent with physical reality. A flower can be as tall as the character if the episode calls for it. A wall can be twice their height or five times their height depending on how imposing it needs to feel. A puddle can be ankle-width or stretch to the horizon. This flexibility is a narrative tool, not a continuity error: the Director should calibrate scale to the emotional weight of the obstacle. Large-scale obstacles should be used when an episode needs to feel more dramatic; small-scale obstacles make episodes feel lighter and more playful. The bird may be the same size as the character's head or twice their full height — both are funny for different reasons. Second characters may be slightly smaller or slightly larger than the main character — differences in size are never used to imply hierarchy or power, only visual variety and comedy. Scale within a single episode should be internally consistent: once an obstacle is established at a given size, it does not change.
