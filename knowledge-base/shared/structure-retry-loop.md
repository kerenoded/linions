# structure-retry-loop.md

When a viewer chooses the failing path, the episode enters a retry loop for that act: the fail clip plays, the character recovers, and the viewer is returned to the same choice moment. Each retry loop iteration should feel slightly different even if the choices are identical: the character's body language on the approach to the choice is more worn after two fails than after one, and their fourth-wall acknowledgement becomes more pointed with each cycle. The fail clip itself must be complete and watchable on its own — a viewer who chooses the fail option repeatedly should be entertained by the sequence, not bored by it. The retry loop does not time out or force the viewer onto the win path: they can fail indefinitely, and the episode accommodates this with good grace. However, the episode's design should make the winning choice feel increasingly obvious after one or two fails — the retry loop's comedy depends on escalating obviousness, not genuine confusion. The act only advances when the winning choice is selected.
