# arc-setup-obstacle-resolution.md

Every episode follows a three-part arc: the character floats with purpose, something stops them, and a choice determines whether they get through. The setup float is relaxed and self-contained — the character is in a good mood, their pace is comfortable, and the world feels ordinary; this normalcy is what makes the obstacle's arrival feel disruptive. The obstacle is presented without explanation — it simply exists on the line, as if it always has — and the character reacts to it honestly without contextualising it for the viewer. The resolution is viewer-driven: two choices are offered, one leads to a clear win and one leads to a clear fail with the same emotional honesty. After resolution, regardless of outcome, the float continues into the next act's setup and the tone resets within a few seconds. An episode should feel like a complete small journey even when replayed on a different branch: every path has a beginning, a complication, and a landing.
