# structure-branching-choices.md

Each act presents two or three labelled choices to the viewer, exactly one of which is the winning path and the rest lead to a short fail-and-retry sequence. Choice labels are action-oriented, specific, and written in the character's world — not abstract options but concrete things they could do: "Use the plank," "Climb the tree," "Wait and listen." The winning choice is not always the obviously sensible one: sometimes the creative or unconventional option wins to reward curious viewers who experiment. The fail choices must lead to outcomes that are clearly resolved — the character cannot be permanently stuck — so every fail path ends with a natural setup for trying again. The retry path always offers the same choices again; the viewer can keep choosing the fail option intentionally and the character will keep attempting it with diminishing dignity and increasing fourth-wall awareness. A choice should feel meaningful: both options look plausible enough that the viewer actually thinks before choosing.
